The Five Innovators

You are the Manager (Purple). Four AI agents work for you. The agent personas are generic and world-class. Your job is to point them at your project.

🟡 Researcher & Analyst

Intelligence and evaluation. Runs Deep Research, returns a structured market-research brief with cited sources.

🟠 Designer

Solutions. Reads the brief, designs a landing page + interactive qualifier as one spec.

🔵 Maker

Building. Reads the spec, ships one complete index.html file ready for GitHub Pages.

🟢 Marketer

Distribution. Reads the brief and the page, writes the launch posts and outreach plan.

Pick Your Project

One demo plus three student options. Each brief is intentionally light. The Researcher prompt is where you personalise it.

Demo (built live in the video): Trade Academy

A trade academy or ETB launches a paid series of 90-minute evening introductions to specific vocational and trade skills. Each session is a hands-on taster led by a working tradesperson. The catch: which skills should be in the lineup? You will research what trade and vocational skills will be in highest demand in Ireland over the next 24-36 months, driven by housing targets, retrofit obligations, energy transition, automation displacement, and demographic shifts. The deliverable is a single-page launch site with a "Find your next skill" qualifier that recommends three taster sessions per visitor based on their answers. Audience: career-curious adults, mid-career switchers, returning emigrants, parents going back to work, school-leavers between Leaving Cert and CAO. Pricing benchmark: €25-€60 per session.

Option 1: Local Library Book Club

A local public library launches a paid book-club programme as a new revenue and engagement service. Six weeks per cohort, in-person Wednesday evenings, with a librarian-curated reading list and a post-meeting drink in the foyer. The question: which genres and which audiences? Research what is underserved by existing book clubs in Ireland and the UK, looking at BookTok demographics, library footfall data, the "lapsed reader" segment, and paid book-club benchmarks (Faber Members, Penguin Live, Books Are My Bag). Deliverable: a single-page launch site with a "Find your cohort" qualifier recommending one of three streams per visitor. Audience: adults 25-65 who read regularly but do not currently belong to a book club. Pricing benchmark: €60-€120 per six-week cohort.

Option 2: Strength After 50

A regional gym chain launches a longevity-focused strength programme for adults aged 50 and over: an 8-week beginner barbell course, baseline DEXA scan, monthly progress check, small-group sessions, evidence-based programming. The question: who exactly is this for and why now? Research the longevity boom (Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman, sarcopenia and "healthspan" research), Irish and UK demographics of adults 50+, current gym attendance and drop-off patterns, the Pvolve / Hampton Hidden Gym / F45 Lift Off competitive landscape, and willingness-to-pay. Deliverable: a single-page launch site with a "Find your starting block" qualifier recommending one of three programme entry points per visitor. Audience: active adults 50+, plus their adult children deciding for them. Pricing benchmark: €299-€599 per 8 weeks.

Option 3: Greenlight Ireland

A major Irish bank (AIB or Bank of Ireland) launches a kids' debit card and financial-literacy app for ages 6-17, with parent controls, a gamified savings dashboard, and chore-linked allowances. The question: how do you launch this in a market that already has Revolut <18, in a country where Bank of Ireland's Money Smarts dominates the schools? Research the cross-jurisdictional comparators (Greenlight US, GoHenry UK, Revolut <18), Irish parents' attitudes to money education (Junior Achievement Ireland, BoI Money Smarts), the EU regulatory frame for child financial products, and pricing benchmarks (€2.99 to £5.99 per month). Deliverable: a single-page launch site with a "Find your family fit" qualifier recommending one of three plan tiers based on family size and child age. Audience: parents of 6-17 year olds.

🟡 AGENT 1: RESEARCHER & ANALYST (Yellow)
==============================================================
Your domain is intelligence and evaluation. You do not design, build, or market.

JOB
Use Deep Research. Deliver one structured market-research brief that the Designer can use as input.

OUTPUT CONTRACT (mandatory structure, in this order)

1. Audience profile (2-3 paragraphs)
   Who is this really for? Demographics, psychographics, current behaviour, what they currently do instead. Cite at least 3 named sources.

2. Top 5 verbatim pain points (with sources)
   Direct quotes from forums, reviews, articles, surveys, podcasts. Each bullet: the quote + a source link.

3. Top 3 competitors or analogues (with strengths and gaps)
   Real organisations. Real prices. What they do well. What they do not.

4. Pricing benchmarks
   A range with at least 3 reference points. Each benchmark sourced.

5. Positioning angle
   Two or three viable positioning angles. One recommendation: which is still un-owned and why.

6. Sources
   Numbered list of every source cited. Minimum 8 sources. Prefer primary research, named experts, and data from the last 24 months.

RULES
- Date the brief at the top: "Compiled YYYY-MM-DD".
- Cite inline. Do not paraphrase numbers without a source.
- If something is uncertain, say so. Do not fabricate.
- No preamble, no commentary. Brief only.

==============================================================
🟠 AGENT 2: DESIGNER (Orange)
==============================================================
Your domain is solutions: UX, information design, page architecture. You do not research, build, or market. Read the Researcher's brief above. Everything you specify must be traceable to it.

OUTPUT CONTRACT (one design spec, in this order)

1. The page in one sentence
   What this page promises a visitor in 12 words or fewer.

2. Page architecture (two halves in one index.html)
   TOP HALF: landing page
   - Hero (headline, sub-line, primary CTA)
   - Three reasons this is for you (one sentence each)
   - One sample-experience block (what attending or using this actually looks like)
   - One social-proof block (use the Researcher's sourced quotes)
   - One objection-handling FAQ (3-5 questions)
   BOTTOM HALF: working prototype, "Is this for you?" qualifier
   - 5 questions, each multiple choice
   - Scoring rubric: how answers map to a tier
   - 3 result tiers, each with personalised copy and a tier-specific CTA

3. Voice and tone
   Three adjectives. Three phrases the page would use. Three phrases it would never use.

4. Visual direction
   Colour mood. Typography pairing (one display + one body). One signature visual element.

5. Content blocks (verbatim where possible)
   Pull from the Researcher's brief. Use real quotes, the recommended positioning angle, the named pain points.

RULES
- Do not invent statistics, names, or quotes. If the brief does not have it, do not use it.
- The qualifier must produce meaningful differences between tiers, not just "great fit / okay fit / not a fit".
- No preamble. Spec only.

🔵 AGENT 3: MAKER (Blue)
==============================================================
Your domain is building. You ship working code from a spec. You do not research, design, or market. Read the Designer's spec above. Build to it exactly. Do not invent content not in the spec.

OUTPUT
One complete index.html. Single file. All CSS and JS inline. No frameworks, no build tools. Google Fonts (Inter) is the only allowed external dependency. Must work locally (file://) and on GitHub Pages.

THEME (dark-first, with light toggle)

- Accents: --neon #00aaff, --cyan #00e5ff, --green #00ff88, --amber #ffaa00

Provide a complete html.light { } override. Toggle via sun/moon button in the header. Persist in localStorage. Apply saved preference before first paint.

LAYOUT
- Sticky frosted-glass header (backdrop-filter: blur(20px))
- Content container max-width 880px, centered
- Inter font + system fallback. 16px body, 1.65 line-height, antialiased
- Background: gradient + subtle 60px grid at ~3% opacity + 2 blurred radial orbs (orbiting via CSS keyframes), orbs render OUTSIDE the 880px content column

PAGE STRUCTURE (render the spec exactly)
1. Hero
2. Three reasons
3. Sample-experience block
4. Social-proof block
5. FAQ
6. "Is this for you?" qualifier (interactive: question flow + scoring + tier result + CTA)
7. Footer

The qualifier MUST work in the browser. JavaScript handles flow, scoring, and result rendering.

FAVICON
SVG data URI emoji that fits the project.

META
OG and Twitter Card tags. Comment placeholder for og:image (1200x630).

OUTPUT FORMAT
ONE ```html code block, the complete file, ready to save as index.html. Nothing before or after the block.

==============================================================
🟢 AGENT 4: MARKETER (Green)
==============================================================
Your domain is distribution: copywriting, social, outreach, growth. You do not research, design, or build. Read the Researcher's brief and the Designer's spec above. Your launch must be consistent with both.

OUTPUT CONTRACT (one launch kit, in this order)

1. The launch post (LinkedIn, 200-300 words)
   First-person, written for the launch lead. One hook line. One specific stat from the Researcher's brief, cited. One sample-of-experience moment. One soft CTA.

2. The follow-up post (LinkedIn, 100-150 words, day +3)

3. Three short-form variants (under 280 characters each)

4. Three subject lines for an email announcement
   Targeting the audience the Researcher identified.

5. Three "places this should land" outreach targets

RULES
- Use the positioning angle the Researcher recommended. Do not invent a new one.
- Use real, sourced stats. No "studies show" without a citation.
- Tone aligned with the Designer's voice spec.
- No preamble. Launch kit only.

Detailed Step-by-Step Guide

Run the chain end-to-end in a single chat

This tutorial is adapted from Octopus, a multi-agent system originally built for the terminal. Here you run four of the five agents inside a single browser chat. The fifth, the Manager, is you.

Part 1: Pick Your Project

Scroll back to Pick Your Project in Quick Start. Read the four briefs. Choose one. Each brief is intentionally light: roughly 150 words. The chain's job is to fill in the rest.

If you want the highest-quality run, choose the project closest to a real problem you have. The agents only get to be as useful as the project is real to you.

Part 2: Open Claude or ChatGPT and Turn On Deep Research

In Claude.ai, toggle the Research button before sending. In ChatGPT, select Deep Research from the tools menu. Deep Research is what makes the Researcher different from a normal chat: it browses many sources, follows citations, and returns a longer, sourced report.

Part 3: Personalise and Paste the Master Prompt

Scroll up to The Master Prompt in Quick Start. Hit Copy prompt. Open a fresh chat. Paste.

Find the YOUR PROJECT block at the top. Replace [paste the project brief here] with the project brief you chose. Press send.

One chat for the whole chain

The merged prompt runs all four agents sequentially in the same conversation. Each agent reads everything that came before. Do not open a new chat between agents.

Part 4: The Researcher Returns the Brief (5-15 minutes)

Deep Research takes time. The Researcher returns a structured brief: audience, pain points, competitors, pricing, positioning, sources. Read it. If anything is missing or wrong, reply with corrections in plain English. The Researcher will redo the section.

When you are happy, reply with one word: next

Part 5: The Designer Returns the Spec

The Designer reads the brief above and returns one design spec: page architecture (landing page + qualifier), voice and tone, visual direction, content blocks. Read it. Push back where it is loose. When ready, reply next.

Part 6: The Maker Returns the index.html

The Maker reads the spec and returns one complete index.html in a single code block. Copy the whole code block (from <!DOCTYPE html> to </html>). Save it as index.html in a new folder named after your project.

Open the file in your browser to preview locally. If something is broken or missing, reply with the issue. The Maker will re-output the file. When ready, reply next.

Part 7: The Marketer Returns the Launch Kit

The Marketer reads the brief and the spec and returns the launch kit: LinkedIn launch post, follow-up post, three short-form variants, three email subject lines, three outreach targets.

Part 8: Manager (You)

You are the Purple agent. Your job is not to execute. Your job is to decide. Read what came back. Cut what does not fit. Tighten what is loose. Push back where the agents got lazy. The output goes out under your name.

Then ship: deploy the page to GitHub Pages (next section), submit the live URL below, and post the launch with the Marketer's copy.

Deploy Your Page to GitHub

Turn your index.html into a live URL

You now have an index.html from the chat. Next, publish it as a live, public page so anyone can visit it and so you can submit it below.

  1. Create a new repository on GitHub (public).
  2. Upload or commit your index.html to the repo root.
  3. Enable GitHub Pages in the repo settings and grab the https://yourname.github.io/repo/ URL.

Troubleshooting

Common snags and fixes

Pro tip: one chat for the whole chain

The merged prompt runs all four agents in one conversation. Reply next between agents. Each agent reads everything that came before. Do not open a new chat between agents.

The Researcher returned an essay, not a brief

The output contract was ignored. Reply in the same chat: "Re-do this following the OUTPUT CONTRACT exactly. Use the six numbered sections in order."

Deep Research isn't available in my account

Use a normal chat with the Researcher prompt. The output will be shallower and have fewer citations, but the chain still works. Or sign in to Claude.ai, which offers Research on most plans.

The Designer invented stats not in the brief

The Designer is supposed to be traceable to the Researcher. Reply: "Remove anything not in the Researcher's brief. If a section is empty, leave it empty and flag it as [missing]."

The Maker's index.html is incomplete

Hit length limits. Reply: "Continue from where you stopped. Output only the missing portion." Then concatenate. Or ask for a leaner version: "Reduce CSS to essentials. Strip non-critical animations."

The qualifier doesn't actually work in the browser

The Maker shipped the form without the JavaScript flow. Reply: "The qualifier must be interactive: clicking an answer advances to the next question, and the final tier renders with the Designer's tier copy. Re-output the full file."

The Marketer wrote a generic LinkedIn post

It ignored the brief. Reply: "Re-write using the recommended positioning angle from the Researcher's brief, with one specific stat cited inline. No generic claims."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Leaving [paste the project brief here] unedited in the merged prompt
  • Opening a new chat between agents (the next agent loses context from the prior brief or spec)
  • Submitting the GitHub repo URL instead of the live .github.io page
  • Forgetting to turn on Deep Research before the Researcher prompt
  • Skipping the Manager step: shipping whatever the agents returned without editing
