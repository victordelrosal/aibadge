# marketing.md: 🟢 Cathal, lifecycle + BookTok launch copy

**Campaign:** Inkwell Rewritten
**The one metric every asset drives:** emails captured from under-35 readers.
**Single CTA everywhere:** "Claim your Library Card" → the sign-up landing page (the three.js "Infinite Library").
**The spine:** the lapsed reader starts a new chapter. The paper stamp card becomes a digital Library Card that earns a free book and unlocks members-only late-night author events.
**The only headline stat permitted (computed in diagnosis.md):** in two years, under-35 readers fell from 35% to 23% of our counter. Used plainly, never inflated.

---

## 1. The launch email

**Who it goes to / framing.** Inkwell has no list yet (thirty years of loyal readers, zero email addresses). So this is not a blast to a cold list. It is the **welcome / win-back email that the Library Card sign-up triggers**: the first thing a reader receives the moment they claim their card on the landing page. It is the door closing softly behind them as they step back in.

**Subject:** Your Library Card is ready (chapter two starts now)

**Preview / preheader:** A free book, late-night author nights, and no rubber stamp in sight.

**Body (~190 words):**

Welcome back. Or, if this is your first time through our doors in a while, welcome in.

Here is something we should admit: in two years, under-35 readers fell from 35% to 23% of our counter. People who grew up in our shops, three deep at the till on a Saturday, quietly drifted off. We think we know why. We never gave you a reason to remember us, and we never once wrote to you. That ends with this email.

You have just claimed your Library Card. It lives on your phone, not in a drawer. Here is what it does:

- Earns you a free book. Read nine, the tenth is on us, counted automatically.
- Unlocks members-only late-night author events: the shop after hours, a glass of something, the writer in the room.
- Keeps you in the loop on what our booksellers are actually pressing into people's hands this month.

No algorithm. No 11pm checkout. Just the shop you liked, finally able to say hello.

See you between the shelves.

**CTA button:** Open your Library Card → landing page (the sign-up / member page)

**GDPR-clean footer:**

You are receiving this because you signed up for an Inkwell Books Library Card and ticked the box to hear from us. We email news, recommendations, and event invitations. Nothing else, and never your details to anyone.

Not for you? Unsubscribe here (one click, no questions) and we will not email you again.

Inkwell Books, Camden Street, Dublin 2, Ireland. hello@inkwellbooks.ie

## 2. Three platform-native posts

### Instagram (feed / carousel)

**Target metric:** emails captured from under-35 readers (warm-discovery → "Claim your Library Card").

**Format:** 4-card carousel.
- Card 1: an ink drop blooming into the Inkwell logo on warm parchment. Single line of type: "Inkwell, rewritten."
- Card 2: a shelf-talker in a bookseller's real handwriting: "The stamp card was in a drawer. We get it."
- Card 3: the gold ex-libris Library Card, glowing, mid-flip, "Member since 2026."
- Card 4: the three unlocks as a clean list, plus "Claim your Library Card. Link in bio."

**Caption:**

You only remember we exist when you walk past the window. Fair. We never gave you a way to keep us in your pocket.

So we made one. The old paper stamp card is now a Library Card that lives on your phone. It earns you a free book, and it gets you into our members-only late-night author nights: the shop after hours, the writer in the room.

No app store, no 11pm checkout, no algorithm deciding what you read next. Just our booksellers, doing the thing Amazon structurally cannot.

Start your next chapter. Claim your Library Card (link in bio).

**Asset note:** carousel in Ink Navy / Warm Parchment, Fraunces for display. Gold foil appears once, on the card. Alt text on every card.

### TikTok / Reels (BookTok-native, the under-25 discovery channel)

**Target metric:** emails captured from under-25 readers (BookTok is where this cohort discovers; this is the top of the funnel for the one metric).

**Audio note:** a quiet, trending "cosy academia" or lo-fi piano sound; let the bookseller's voice sit on top. No hard sell, no countdown.

**Shot-by-shot (vertical, ~22s):**

- 0:00–0:02 (HOOK). Close on a hand pulling a creased, forgotten paper stamp card out of a kitchen drawer. On-screen text: "Found my Inkwell card. It has 2 stamps. From 2021."
- 0:02–0:06. A bookseller (real, warm, mid-shelving) glances at camera. On-screen text: "We retired these."
- 0:06–0:11. Phone lifts into frame; the gold Library Card glows on screen and flips. On-screen text: "It lives here now. Earns you a free book."
- 0:11–0:16. Quick handheld push through the shop at night, fairy lights, a few people, a writer reading aloud. On-screen text: "And gets you into our late-night author nights. Members only."
- 0:16–0:20. Back to the bookseller, sliding a romantasy/YA pick across the counter. On-screen text: "The shelf your For You page is obsessed with. We have it."
- 0:20–0:22 (CTA). Cut to parchment card: "Claim your Library Card. Link in bio." Voice: "Start your next chapter."

**Asset note:** shot in-store, natural light then candlelight, no studio polish (handmade is the brand). Captions burned in for sound-off viewing. Link in bio → landing page.

### Facebook (the dormant over-50 base)

**Target metric:** emails captured from under-35 readers, **reached secondhand**. This is a secondary channel: the over-50s are loyal and need nothing, but they raised, and live with, the twenty-somethings we have lost. The ask is to pass it along.

**Caption:**

A note for our longest-standing readers, the ones who never left.

You know we are a different shop when someone under thirty is browsing the fiction table. Lately there are fewer of them: in two years, under-35 readers fell from 35% to 23% of our counter. We would like them back, and we have finally built a way to stay in touch with people that does not involve a rubber stamp.

It is called the Library Card. It lives on a phone, earns a free book, and unlocks our new members-only late-night author events.

Here is our ask: tell the twenty-something in your life. The daughter, the nephew, the one who reads on their phone at midnight. Send them this: they can claim their Library Card in under a minute (link below).

Thirty years in, we are writing the next chapter. We would love them in it.

**Asset note:** single warm photo, a real shelf-talker beside the glowing card, Ink Navy / Parchment. Link → landing page. Clear, large, mobile-legible.
